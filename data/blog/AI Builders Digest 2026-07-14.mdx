PODCAST

No Priors: How Nuclear Will Unlock Energy Abundance with Valar Atomics Founder Isaiah Taylor

Valar Atomics founder Isaiah Taylor argues that nuclear has never had its Ford moment — we built in the civil infrastructure era, not the manufacturing era. The industry stopped building reactors after 3 Mile Island and turned into a modeling-and-simulation business producing paper reactors. Valor goes the hardware-iteration route: they built a 100kW TRISO reactor behind us, turned it on under DOE authority via EO 14301, and just powered an AI chip off it. Key insight: when you stop an industry, the skill set atrophies. The US went from being great at large-scale civil infrastructure to advanced manufacturing. The next nuclear buildout looks nothing like the sixties — manufactured, not constructed, with passive safety systems that eliminate the need for active cooling.

Isaiah traces this to his great-grandfather on the Manhattan Project. Energy is the fundamental input: as AI and robotics enable semi-autonomous manufacturing, the cost of everything becomes the cost of the energy used to make it. Figure out how to make energy 10x cheaper, and the demand is set by the price.

A powerful quote from Isaiah: When you shut a reactor off, decay heat is five to six percent of continuous power. In traditional plants, cooling systems must never fail. Valor makes active cooling unnecessary through the geometry of the system itself: water boils, steam leaves, condenses, and natural circulation removes heat with zero moving parts over two days.
